# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Delivery Leads

### Role Summary
Delivery Leads coordinate release planning, handoffs, and deployment readiness. They make sure delivery milestones are aligned across teams and that release execution follows the defined process.

### Responsibilities
- Define and manage release scope, schedule, and readiness criteria
- Coordinate deployment activities across development, QA, and operations
- Prepare release documentation, runbooks, and rollback plans
- Communicate release status, risks, and decisions to Project Managers and Product Managers

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, predictable releases
- Reduce deployment risk and downtime
- Maintain clear accountability for release readiness

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews and deployment coordination meetings
- Release notes, runbooks, and launch status summaries
- Cross-team release planning and handoff sessions

---

## QA / Quality Assurance Leads

### Role Summary
QA Leads own quality assurance strategy, test execution planning, and validation of acceptance criteria. They ensure the team delivers quality outcomes that meet product and compliance expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategies, quality gates, and validation requirements
- Validate that acceptance criteria are complete and testable
- Coordinate defect triage, regression testing, and release validation
- Work with Developers, Product Managers, and Project Managers to align quality activities with delivery plans

### Goals
- Ensure work meets defined quality standards
- Detect and resolve issues early in the delivery cycle
- Improve confidence in release readiness

### Typical Communication
- Test planning and quality review sessions
- Defect status reports and validation summaries
- Collaboration with development and product teams on acceptance criteria

---

## Stakeholder Liaisons

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Liaisons represent business interests, support decision-making, and keep stakeholders aligned with project priorities and outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain stakeholder alignment on goals, scope, and timelines
- Escalate risks, decisions, and dependencies when needed
- Support Product Managers with business context and approval decisions
- Communicate stakeholder feedback to the delivery team

### Goals
- Keep business and delivery expectations aligned
- Reduce scope misunderstandings and late changes
- Improve stakeholder confidence in project outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder status updates and alignment briefings
- Escalation and decision support meetings
- Feedback loops with product and project leaders

---

## Technical Architects

### Role Summary
Technical Architects guide solution design, manage technical dependencies, and ensure architecture consistency across teams.

### Responsibilities
- Define architecture approaches and technology standards
- Review proposed designs and identify cross-team dependencies
- Support Developers with technical guidance and risk identification
- Coordinate with Project Managers to balance technical and delivery constraints

### Goals
- Keep architecture aligned with long-term strategy
- Reduce technical debt and integration risk
- Ensure delivery decisions reflect sound technical direction

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review sessions and design workshops
- Technical dependency mapping and alignment meetings
- Collaboration with development teams on technical trade-offs

---

## Change Managers

### Role Summary
Change Managers oversee change control, approvals, and communication related to project scope, delivery plans, and releases.

### Responsibilities
- Manage change request processes and governance checks
- Assess impact of scope or schedule changes on delivery plans
- Coordinate approval workflows with Project Managers, Delivery Leads, and Stakeholder Liaisons
- Communicate change decisions and updated expectations to the team

### Goals
- Keep changes controlled and transparent
- Minimize delivery disruption from scope shifts
- Ensure changes are processed with the right stakeholder input

### Typical Communication
- Change review and approval meetings
- Impact assessments and updated plan communication
- Coordination with delivery and stakeholder teams
